Today's Focus · The IN / ON / AT Funnel

Zoom lens rule: IN = big span (year, month, season) → ON = a specific day → AT = an exact point (a clock time). The more specific the time, the "smaller" the preposition.

PrepUse forExamples
ATclock times · mealtimes · fixed points · night at 7 o'clock, at noon, at midnight, at night, at lunchtime, at the weekend (BrE), at Christmas, at the moment
ONdays · dates · a specific day · day + part of day on Monday, on 5th June, on my birthday, on Christmas Day, on Friday morning, on weekdays, on time
INmonths · years · seasons · centuries · parts of the day · long periods in April, in 2026, in summer, in the morning, in the 21st century, in the holidays, in two days (= after two days)

Watch out (common learner errors)

  • at night BUT in the morning / afternoon / evening.
  • Day + part of day → use ON: on Monday morning (not "in Monday morning").
  • No preposition before last, next, this, every, tomorrow, yesterday: "See you next Monday" (not "on next Monday").
  • in time = with time to spare ≠ on time = punctual / as scheduled.
